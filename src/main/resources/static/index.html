Uncovering the Signal in the Noise

Current suspicious activity identification is passive & focused on remaining compliant. This focus results in monitoring solutions that primarily produces false positive alerts and cases, hiding real risks within financial payment networks.

Risks and threats are difficult to discern in networks that include multiple currencies, lines of business, and and firms, allowing entities to proclaim to be one type of customer, but behave as another.

Pictured Right: SWIFT MT 103 Payment network provided by SWIFT-SCOPE

Not just who transacts with who, But who should be transacting across networks?

FNA’s anomaly detection for entities and transactions enhances and augments existing suspicious transaction and entity risk scoring.

Deep neural network link prediction adds an additional layer to existing suspicious scores. We are able to learn how the graph evolves and to signal transactions with a low probability of happening as anomalous; namely, those which are not conforming with the network historical payment patterns.

Pictured left: The data input is enriched with graph features, the result is a probability for each possible link in your financial payment system

Dynamic KYC / Segmentation Optimization: Are you who you say you are?

Are the entities participating in suspicious and anomalous transactions who or what they purport to be?

We are able to derive significant features from the fingerprints of the transactions of each organisation. We can use this fingerprint to identify entities in misrepresented segments due to a mismatch in expected behavior, resulting from either business adaptation or potential criminal activity.

Payment system participants leave fingerprints

With FNA, you can reduce high density data laden with false positive risks to high fidelity data with high probability of risk, and seamlessly launch an investigation into entities of interest with leveraging multiple data sources.
