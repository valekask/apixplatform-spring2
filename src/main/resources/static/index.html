Uncovering the Signal in the Noise

In an increasingly interconnected world payment networks include multiple currencies, lines of business, and firms, allowing entities to proclaim to be one type of customer, but behave as another.

This makes it easier for real risks and threats to hide - whilst challenging the monitoring solutions to proactively identify the right signals in near real time.

Pictured Right: SWIFT MT 103 Payment network provided by SWIFT-SCOPE.

Not just who transacts with who, But who should be transacting across networks?

FNA’s anomaly detection for entities and transactions enhances and augments existing suspicious transaction and entity risk scoring.

Deep neural network link prediction adds an additional layer to existing suspicious scores. This enables us to follow how the graph evolves and to signal transactions with a low probability of happening as anomalous; namely, those which are not conforming with historical payment patterns.

Pictured left: The data input is enriched with graph features, the result is a probability for each possible link in your financial payment system.

Dynamic KYC / Segmentation Optimization: Are You Who You Say You Are?

Are the entities participating in suspicious and anomalous transactions who or what they purport to be?

FNA’s Detect and Investigate Suite has been designed to derive significant features from the fingerprints of the transactions of each organisation. We can use these fingerprints to identify entities in misrepresented segments due to a mismatch in expected behavior, resulting from either business adaptation or potential criminal activity.

Payment system participants leave fingerprints

With FNA, you can reduce high density data laden with false positive risks to high fidelity data with high probability of risk, and seamlessly launch an investigation into entities of interest and leverage multiple data sources. Crypto exchanges can also be identified and singled out for their involvement in suspicious transactions.

Pictured Left: Network data reduced to highly suspicious anomalous transactions with crypto exchanges and a possible unlicensed exchange.
